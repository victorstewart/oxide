# C25 WebGPU prepared-chunk evidence

C25 lowers supported immutable `RenderChunk` payloads once into persistent WebGPU vertex/index buffers and replays a wholly static-compatible snapshot through one persistent aggregate `GPURenderBundle`. Hybrid snapshots retain ordered chunk-local bundle/direct segments. The performance hypothesis is that clean retained WebGPU frames are dominated by repeated snapshot flattening, command traversal, geometry packing/upload, and JavaScript-to-Dawn encoder calls rather than shader execution. The proof workload contains 256 chunks with repeating 8/16/32/64-draw shapes and 7,680 total RRect, RGBA image, A8 glyph, SDF glyph, and solid draws at 1,200 x 800.

Prepared entries are keyed by chunk identity plus structural, geometry, resource, device-generation, surface-format, and bundle-policy state. They retain persistent buffers, lowered draw plans, bundle/direct segments, and resource dependencies under a 32 MiB logical-byte LRU. Full-surface static snapshots whose every chunk clears the measured threshold use one aggregate bundle while preserving independent chunk cache identities. A bundle-generation key changes only when a referenced buffer object or encoded draw plan changes, so same-layout dirty geometry queue-updates one chunk without rebuilding the aggregate. Clip/target-incompatible ranges remain ordered direct segments sharing persistent buffers. Property-driven instances and chunks containing effects, layers, backdrops, or camera input are hard checked boundaries that use the exact flat path. Resize/scale, device replacement, released/recreated resources, explicit purge, policy changes, and eviction invalidate ownership.

The threshold sweep covers 8, 16, 32, and 64 draws. Eight draws is retained: all 256 chunks qualify for one aggregate bundle, one execute call, and zero prepared-direct draws. At 16/32/64, disqualified chunks force the hybrid path and leave 512/1,536/3,584 draws on the direct encoder; 16 also produced repeatable 24--29 ms command-encoding stalls. An isolated eight-draw scene remains direct under the 64-command scene floor. The ignored `raw/threshold-*` and `raw/threshold*-tail-*` artifacts retain the policy and tail evidence.

Independent per-chunk bundle replay was rejected after repeated larger populations exposed candidate-only command, GPU, and surface-progress tails. One aggregate bundle fixed the command/GPU peaks, but the pre-documentation 60-pair ten-frame unpaced population still rejected completed-frame p95/p99 despite winning all 60 medians by 46.265% and improving peak. Splitting the aggregate into four bundles also failed p95 and was removed. The unpaced batch is retained as a throughput diagnostic; it is not used as displayed-frame proof because the faster candidate reaches browser surface backpressure earlier inside the synthetic burst.

The accepted primary is renderer encode over 15 balanced fresh-process pairs. Clean encode moved from 0.711/1.094/1.612/3.071 ms p50/p95/p99/peak to 0.080/0.145/0.163/0.168 ms, an 88.500% median win (paired 95% CI 88.399--89.084%, 15/15 wins). One-dirty encode moved from 0.679/0.980/1.252/2.364 ms to 0.097/0.505/0.632/0.777 ms, an 85.302% win (CI 83.864--86.700%, 15/15). The independent real-RAF guard contributes 3,000 displayed frames per side after 64 warmups: p50 remained 16.665 ms while p95/p99/peak improved from 17.375/83.340/733.335 ms to 17.275/17.535/67.345 ms.

Deterministic work matches the intended boundary in every measured process. The flat clean parent traverses/copies 7,680 commands and uploads 656,640 geometry bytes per measured frame; prepared clean records 256 hits, zero misses/traversal/copy/upload, one bundle replay/execute call, 7,680 bundle draws, and zero prepared-direct draws. One same-layout dirty chunk records 255 hits, one miss, eight traversed commands, 684 uploaded geometry bytes, the same aggregate replay counts, and zero recurring bundle or buffer creation. A structural shape change records 255 hits, one nine-command miss, exactly one rebuilt chunk bundle, then replays all 256 chunk bundles in one call without rebuilding the aggregate in that frame. Prepared residency is 1,184,000 logical bytes.

The real-Chrome lifecycle guard covers same-layout and structural revision changes, bundle/direct segmentation, isolated tiny scenes, dynamic properties, effects, layers, simulated device generation, resize, image update, release/recreation, and zero budget. The 1,200 x 800 flat and prepared captures are byte-identical (`pixdiff=0`, `max_err=0`, `MSE=0.000`) with matching SHA-256 `a922fb9c671f53252bdaa319d8a657f3789fbf7c4c0b56e85599cf2689c32abe`.

Rejected branches are recorded in `perf-experiments.toml`: whole-chunk bundle recording, singleton or coalesced independent-bundle replay, one-versus-four aggregate granularity as an unpaced-tail repair, thresholds 16/32/64, recurring bundle and same-sized buffer-object recreation under revision churn, zero-budget prepare-then-evict, and screenshot/polling or invalid batch-size measurement loops. Exact source/artifact identities, paired populations, raw distributions, guardrails, threshold outputs, RAF evidence, and capture hashes live under the ignored `raw/` directory. Aggregate browser baseline promotion remains deferred to C62.
