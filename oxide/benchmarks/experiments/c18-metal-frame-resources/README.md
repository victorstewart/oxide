# C18 Metal frame-resource evidence

C18 makes visible Metal hosts own three dynamic frame slots, matching their normal safe in-flight depth, while preserving a separately selected eight-slot offscreen/perf mode. Slot reuse is governed by one completion-owned bitset: submission sets its slot bit and the Metal completion handler clears it. Selection loads the bitset once and scans from the next slot without division or modulo. The renderer does not infer resource lifetime from `CAMetalLayer.maximumDrawableCount`, retain a second command-buffer reference only for slot tracking, or wait when all configured slots remain in flight.

The performance hypothesis is that eight unconditional 4/2/2 MiB vertex/index/uniform buffer sets retain far more shared memory than visible UI needs. The affected stages are renderer construction, dynamic frame-resource residency, first workload growth, auxiliary per-frame ownership, and event-to-visible frame submission. The target visible high-water workloads are 4,096 quads and 1,024 neon markers; the offscreen stress is 8,192 quads across all eight slots. Expected movement is visible ring storage from 67,108,864 to 2,015,232 Metal-allocated bytes, zero growth at both measured high-water workloads, bounded cold growth in the deeper stress mode, zero warm growth, and no new frame-backpressure skips. The risk is premature reuse of in-flight buffers or hidden growth/backpressure under dense work.

Initial requested capacities are 512 KiB of vertices, 64 KiB of indices, and 72 KiB of uniforms per slot. The permanent visible row reports exact high-water use of 327,680/49,152/16 bytes, depth 3, 2,015,232 retained bytes after Metal allocation rounding, and zero cold/warm growth; the existing 1,024-marker snapshot additionally freezes 73,728 uniform bytes with zero growth. The permanent offscreen row traverses all eight slots with 8,192 quads, reports 16 cold grows, then zero warm grows and zero skips with 7,733,248 post-growth allocated bytes. Renderer tests separately saturate all visible and offscreen slots, prove a nonblocking skip, release a slot, and prove completion-safe reuse.

The production collection-navigation journey supplies the event-to-visible guard. The first three-slot design kept per-slot completion atomics, a redundant retained command-buffer reference, and variable-modulo scanning. Although one exploratory doubled population passed, its later exact staged-tree run failed the 5% peak guard and the design was rejected. Removing only the command-buffer retain improved p50/p95/p99 in its doubled run, but its 0.268541 ms peak exceeded the parent's 0.220417 ms and that design was also rejected.

The completion-bitset redesign first passed the timing gate with a 64 KiB uniform ring, but the existing 1,024-marker workload then exposed a 73,728-byte high-water mark and one first-use growth. That candidate was functionally rejected rather than retaining a misleading timing win. Raising the uniform capacity to the measured 72 KiB removed growth, but the depth-sized heap-`Vec` ring representation failed its permitted doubled 60-pair population: parent/candidate p50/p95/p99/peak were 0.057584/0.070668/0.084625/0.202291 ms and 0.057875/0.073212/0.088423/0.321209 ms.

A fixed `[Option<Buffer>; 8]` representation allocated only active Metal buffers, but its doubled 30-pair population failed p99 by 3.598% despite improving peak. Replacing the option lookup with direct `[Buffer; 8]` access made inactive cells alias slot zero without allocating more Metal storage; p50/p95/p99 then improved, but a 0.204167 ms candidate peak versus 0.146958 ms parent still failed the strict peak guard while frame-resource depth was copied through every `PerfStats` snapshot. Both representations remain rejected.

The retained design keeps direct fixed-buffer access, accounts only configured active slots, isolates growth and alias refresh in a cold non-inlined path, consolidates completion into one `AtomicU8`, removes division from the rotating scan, and reports static depth from the selected configuration instead of per-frame stats. Its first declared 15-pair population accepted with 720 raw samples per side: parent/candidate p50/p95/p99/peak were 0.056958/0.069881/0.087260/0.188666 ms and 0.057667/0.069964/0.082660/0.098959 ms. Paired median speedup was -2.266%, 95% CI -3.438%..3.863%, with 6/15 wins. This is a no-material-regression result rather than a latency win; p95 is effectively flat while p99 and peak improve, and deterministic journey counters report zero growth and backpressure skips.

The locally retained `raw/` directory contains paired plans, hashes, outputs, and raw samples. Official `latest.*` baseline promotion remains deferred to C62.
